Steve Rogers / Captain America

Character Summary

Steve Rogers begins as a determined but physically frail young man who volunteers for a military experiment during World War II and becomes Captain America. After spending decades frozen in the ice, he wakes in the modern world and becomes one of the Avengers' most trusted leaders. Throughout the MCU, Steve serves as the moral centre of the team, though his unwavering principles often bring him into conflict with friends, governments and even fellow heroes.

Thor / God of Thunder

Character Summary

Thor is the Asgardian God of Thunder, a prince raised for glory who gradually learns that being worthy means more than strength, status or victory in battle. His story moves between cosmic war, family loss, exile, grief and reinvention, often with more humour than anyone involved probably planned. Across the MCU, Thor connects Earth’s heroes to the wider universe and remains one of the Avengers’ most powerful and unpredictable allies.

Over time he becomes one of the Avengers' most trusted members, contributing some of the team's most important scientific breakthroughs while gradually finding peace with both sides of himself.

Clint Barton / Hawkeye

Character Summary

Clint Barton is a master archer, former S.H.I.E.L.D. agent and founding Avenger. Unlike many of his teammates, Clint possesses no superpowers, relying instead on exceptional skill, experience and determination. Throughout the MCU he often serves as a reminder of what the heroes are fighting for, balancing the demands of saving the world with his devotion to his family. Character Summary

Peter Parker is a gifted young scientist from Queens who gains spider-like abilities after being bitten by a radioactive spider. Balancing school, friendships and superhero responsibilities, Peter often finds himself carrying burdens far beyond his years.
